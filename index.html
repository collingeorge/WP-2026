Notice
All WP-2026 products are unclassified open-source analytical products produced for educational, research, and policy analysis purposes. Content is derived exclusively from publicly available doctrine, government publications, and open-source academic sources. Analytical frameworks are organizing constructs derived from documented authorities and observed practice. Where the public record does not sustain a firm judgment, the limit is stated explicitly. This platform aggregates analytic frameworks and publicly derived data. It may reduce the effort required to structure analysis but does not provide operational capability, targeting instruction, or executable guidance. The platform does not integrate modules into a unified analytic output. Cross-module synthesis remains the responsibility of the user.

MDO-01 v3.0 · ODNI Two-Axis Standard · All judgments bounded by public record
1
High Confidence · Doctrine-Supported
MDO is almost certainly the governing joint warfighting concept for peer and near-peer conflict, codified in FM 3-0 (2022) and JP 3-0.
2
Moderate Confidence · Analytically Inferred
The three-track framework (Diplomatic / Overt Warfare / Clandestine) likely reflects principal channels of U.S. state power — an analytic construct, not a codified architecture.
3
Low-Moderate Confidence · Analytically Inferred
Multilateral sanctions may impose greater cost than unilateral designation. Whether cost differential produces behavioral change is not established by the open-source record.
4
Low-Moderate Confidence · Analytically Inferred
Submarine forces may offer relatively greater survivability under current ASW conditions. Comparative leverage ranking across asset classes is not asserted.
5
Low-Moderate Confidence · Analytically Inferred
MISO may contribute to competition environment shaping when integrated with other instruments. Isolated causal attribution of behavioral change is not supported by the public record.
6
Low Confidence · Analytically Inferred
Clandestine and covert options may be considered in gray-zone phases. Frequency, effectiveness, and comparative preference are not established in the public record.
Confidence Architecture — ODNI Two-Axis Standard
HIGH — Multiple independent Tier 1 sources; inference minimal
MODERATE — Tier 1/2 sourcing with meaningful inference or unresolved variable
LOW-MODERATE — Structurally sound inference from indirect public reporting
LOW — Primarily analytic inference; reasonable alternative conclusions exist
Platform Architecture

System Architecture and Module Separation

The WP-2026 platform comprises three independent layers. No module is derived from non-public information. No module outputs data to another. Cross-module synthesis remains the responsibility of the user.

Layer 1 — Analytic Framework
MDO-01 v3.0 and the WP-2026 Policy Series
The paper series and MDO-01 console provide doctrinal framing, legal authority mapping, and scenario-conditioned analytic assessments. All content is derived from publicly available doctrine and open-source academic sources. The console organizes analytic considerations — it does not determine, recommend, or prioritize courses of action.
Public-Source Doctrine Console →
Layer 2 — Intelligence Representation
SENI v4 Global Map — 374 Nodes
The SENI map represents publicly documented enforcement-relevant infrastructure nodes derived from open-source analysis. Node ordering reflects the open-source record's observability ceiling — not a validated hierarchy of system-wide strategic effect. This map is a heuristic prioritization construct. It does not output scenario logic and is not coupled to the console or paper series.
Open-Source Representation Map →
Layer 3 — Dissemination Interface
WP-2026 Site and Series Portal
This site provides structured access to the WP-2026 document archive, interactive interfaces, and series standards. It does not aggregate or synthesize outputs from the console or map. No module is dependent on or derived from another. The platform may reduce the effort required to structure analysis but does not provide operational capability, targeting instruction, or executable guidance.
