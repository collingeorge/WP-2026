I. Banking & Correspondent Networks
Node TypeThreat Vector(s)Abuse Mode Red Flags / IndicatorsControlsEscalation Triggers Reporting PathwayAuthority Basis
Correspondent Banking / Nostro Accounts ML / TF / Sanctions Evasion Nested shell co. beneficiaries transit USD/EUR clearing; payable-through accounts obscure originator; bulk cash wire structuring below CTR thresholds
  • No end-beneficiary transparency
  • Respondent bank in FATF grey/black list jurisdiction
  • Rapid in/out same-day flows
  • Inconsistent LEI data
  • Volume increase materially exceeding established customer baseline, consistent with layering typology
  • EDD on respondent
  • Automated wire screening vs. OFAC/UN/EU/FATF lists
  • CDD refresh ≤12 months for high-risk corridors
  • Payable-through account prohibition without sub-account mapping
  • SDN/designated entity match
  • May prompt jurisdictional risk reassessment or de-risking review
  • SAR threshold met based on suspicion, not solely monetary amount (≥$5K indicative)
  • Pattern consistent with ML typology alert
  • SAR → FinCEN (BSA/AML)
  • UN 1267 Committee via OFAC (ISIL/AQ); UNSCR 1373 for TF; UNSCR 2231 for Iran
  • Egmont FIU-to-FIU channel
  • Wolfsberg Group peer notification
 BSA 31 USC §5318 · OFAC SDN/CAPTA · UN SCRs 1267/1373/2231 (scenario-specific) · FATF Rec. 13
Private Banking / Wealth Management ML / Grand Corruption / CPF PEP-linked layering through numbered accounts, discretionary trusts, fiduciary nominee structures; asset concealment for sanctioned oligarchs; family-office opacity
  • PEP designation with unexplained wealth quantum
  • Nominee BO inconsistent with economic rationale
  • Booking center in high-secrecy jurisdiction
  • Refusal to disclose source of wealth
  • Trust/foundation structure with no clear purpose
  • PEP-mandatory EDD including adverse media screening
  • Source-of-wealth verification (documentary)
  • Annual BO re-certification
  • RPEP screening
  • Triggers sanctions review and potential blocking obligations where applicable
  • Match against HM Treasury/OFAC/EU designation list
  • Adverse media hit on corruption/bribery/WMD nexus
  • Incoming from recently sanctioned jurisdiction without apparent lawful purpose or licensing basis
  • SAR → FinCEN/FCA/FINMA depending on booking center
  • FATF mutual evaluation referral
  • DOJ/FBI financial crimes task force referral
 BSA/USA PATRIOT Act §312 · FCPA 15 USC §78dd (corruption nexus only) · UK POCA 2002 §330 · EU AMLD6 Art. 3
Retail / Commercial Bank Accounts Narco-trafficking / TF / ML Structuring (smurfing) below $10K CTR; third-party deposits; cash-intensive business front accounts; funnel accounts; mule network deposits
  • Multiple sub-$10K deposits same branch/day
  • Rapid depletion after deposit (within 24–48h)
  • Account holder inconsistent with transaction volume
  • Multiple 3rd-party depositors
  • Geographic anomaly (deposits in distant states/cities)
  • Automated structuring detection (velocity rules)
  • Cash-intensive business EDD
  • Beneficial ownership CDD (CDD Rule 2018)
  • Geographic clustering analysis
  • Account behavior baseline deviation alerts
  • Repeated structuring patterns within a defined monitoring period
  • Funnel account signature detected
  • Nexus to known narco-trafficking corridor
  • CTR → FinCEN (>$10K cash)
  • SAR → FinCEN
  • DEA/FBI referral via law enforcement liaison, as appropriate based on nexus to narcotics or criminal investigation
 BSA 31 USC §5324 (structuring) · FinCEN CDD Rule 31 CFR §1010.230 · 18 USC §1956
Shell Company / SPV Bank Accounts Sanctions Evasion / CPF / ML Layering through multi-jurisdictional SPV chains; undisclosed beneficial owners using nominee directors; IEEPA evasion via non-US dollar clearing avoidance; round-trip financing
  • No identifiable economic purpose
  • BO resides in sanctioned jurisdiction
  • Incorporation in secrecy jurisdiction within 90 days of account opening
  • Professional nominee director for multiple unrelated entities
  • Inbound funds immediately disbursed to opaque counterparties
  • BO verification to natural person level (≥25% threshold)
  • Negative-news screening on all linked entities
  • GLEIF LEI cross-check
  • Corporate registry verification vs. declared structure
  • Periodic dormancy review
  • BO linked to SDN or sanctioned jurisdiction
  • Multi-layered ownership structure lacking economic or operational rationale
  • Pattern consistent with potential round-tripping typology
  • SAR → FinCEN
  • OFAC administrative subpoena cooperation
  • DOJ NSD referral for IEEPA potential violations
 IEEPA 50 USC §1701 · FinCEN CDD Rule · EU 5th AMLD BO register · FATF Rec. 24/25

Brokerage / Securities Accounts ML / Sanctions Evasion / CPF Wash trading between related accounts; nominee share parking for sanctioned persons; layering via bond purchases then secondary market sale for clean proceeds; short-selling ahead of sanctions designation
  • Circular trading with no net position change
  • BO inconsistency between custodian and issuer records

  • Securities-specific SAR (SAR-SF)
  • Cross-account wash trade surveillance
  • DVP/RVP delivery anomaly monitoring

  • Coordinated account activity suggesting common control, absent legitimate trading strategy explanation
  • SAR-SF → FinCEN
  • SEC Enforcement referral
  • FINRA regulatory referral
  • OFAC reporting per 31 CFR §501.604

OTC Derivatives / Structured Notes CPF / Sanctions Evasion Proliferation finance via structured note issuance in offshore jurisdiction for designated entity affiliate; swap counterparty opacity; CDS referencing sanctioned sovereign debt; offshore derivative booking to avoid SDN contact
  • Counterparty domicile in sanctioned jurisdiction or FATF non-cooperative territory
  • Notional value disproportionate to counterparty balance sheet without documented hedging or risk management rationale
  • Instrument appears designed to reduce exposure to U.S. financial system controls
  • Exotic structure with no apparent hedging rationale
  • ISDA counterparty KYC with BO penetration
  • Derivative booking jurisdiction controls
  • CFTC swap data repository (SDR) reporting
  • Sanctions clause in ISDA Master Agreement
  • Counterparty linked to OFAC/EU-designated entity
  • Instrument provides economic benefit to SDN
  • Instrument appears designed to reduce exposure to U.S. financial system controls
  • SAR → FinCEN
  • CFTC Division of Enforcement referral
  • OFAC ISDA-specific disclosure protocol

III. Virtual Assets & Crypto Infrastructure
Node TypeThreat Vector(s)Abuse ModeRed Flags / IndicatorsControlsEscalation TriggersReporting PathwayAuthority Basis
Centralized VASP / Exchange ML / TF / Sanctions Evasion / Narco Layering through high-volume trades; peer-to-peer exchange to avoid KYC; chain-hopping across blockchains; exchanges in non-FATF-compliant jurisdictions; DPRK Lazarus Group typology (large-scale crypto theft → OTC liquidation)
  • Deposits from OFAC-designated wallet address
  • Transaction volume inconsistent with stated income/purpose
  • Rapid conversion to privacy coin or bridge
  • IP address from sanctioned jurisdiction
  • Cluster analysis links to darknet marketplace
  • Travel Rule compliance (FATF Rec. 16 for VASPs)
  • On-chain blockchain analytics (Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs)
  • OFAC virtual currency compliance framework
  • Address screening against OFAC SDN VA list
  • KYC at onboarding and enhanced for high-value withdrawals
  • Direct exposure to designated wallet cluster
  • Darknet marketplace funds received
  • Blockchain analytics indicators consistent with typologies publicly attributed to DPRK-linked actors
  • Rapid chain-hop pattern post-deposit, in combination with other risk indicators
  • SAR → FinCEN (VASPs are MSBs under BSA)
  • OFAC reporting obligation 31 CFR §501.604
  • FBI/CISA referral for state-actor cyber-enabled theft
  • DOJ CCIPS referral
 BSA 31 USC §5330 (VASP as MSB) · OFAC VA Sanctions Compliance Guidance (2021) · FATF Updated Guidance on VAs (2021) · 18 USC §1960
Privacy Coins / Mixers / Tumblers TF / Narco / ML / CPF Obfuscation of transaction graph via Monero, Zcash shielded pools, or Bitcoin mixers (Tornado Cash typology); DPRK uses Monero for sanctions evasion post-crypto theft; darknet market settlement; NFT wash trading as laundering vehicle
  • Inbound from exchange then immediate mixer deposit
  • Monero receipt address on KYC onboarding
  • NFT self-purchase at inflated value
  • Mixer service deposit without business explanation
  • Output wallet address linked to ransomware cluster
  • Mixer/tumbler service exposure triggers automatic EDD
  • Reject Monero deposits where blockchain tracing not possible per policy
  • NFT platform KYC and transaction monitoring
  • Tornado Cash OFAC designation compliance (Aug 2022)
  • Interaction with OFAC-designated mixer where designation applies (e.g., Tornado Cash, Blender.io)
  • Privacy coin receipt inconsistent with customer profile — elevated risk requiring enhanced review
  • Ransomware cluster linkage
  • SAR → FinCEN
  • OFAC mandatory blocking and reporting
  • FBI Cyber Division referral
  • CISA ransomware reporting portal
 OFAC Tornado Cash designation (OFAC-2022-0831) · BSA 31 USC §5318(g) · 18 USC §1956(a)(1) · FATF Recommendation 15
Crypto OTC Desk (Unregistered) ML / Sanctions Evasion / Narco High-volume cash-to-crypto or crypto-to-fiat conversion without MSB registration; used by narco organizations and sanctions-designated entities to liquidate illicitly obtained crypto; facilitated by operators functionally analogous to informal value transfer systems (UAE, HK, Southeast Asia)
  • No FinCEN MSB registration
  • Transaction volume incompatible with declared business
  • Counterparties in Iran/Russia/DPRK nexus
  • Cash-to-crypto without KYC
  • Operating through messaging platforms (Telegram, WeChat)
  • MSB registration verification at onboarding for institutional OTC counterparties
  • Correspondent crypto-bank due diligence on OTC desk clients
  • On-chain analytics for settlement addresses
  • Travel Rule enforcement at settlement leg
  • Unregistered MSB confirmed
  • Sanctions-nexus counterparty identified
  • Transaction >$25K with no KYC documentation, or otherwise inconsistent with risk-based KYC expectations
  • SAR → FinCEN
  • FinCEN enforcement referral for unlicensed MSB
  • DOJ MLARS referral
  • OFAC penalty process

IV. Real Estate & High-Value Assets
Node TypeThreat Vector(s)Abuse ModeRed Flags / IndicatorsControlsEscalation TriggersReporting PathwayAuthority Basis
Residential / Commercial Real Estate ML / Grand Corruption / Sanctions Evasion All-cash purchases through LLC veil; beneficial owner is sanctioned PEP or narco principal; title held via multi-tier corporate chain; property used as collateral to extract clean debt; FinCEN GTO-covered markets
  • All-cash LLC purchaser with no identifiable BO
  • Property value significantly above market / no negotiation
  • BO in high-corruption / sanctioned-state jurisdiction
  • Serial purchases in FinCEN GTO markets (NYC, Miami, LA, SF, Boston, Seattle, San Antonio, Las Vegas, Dallas, Honolulu)
  • Rapid re-sale at same or lower price
  • FinCEN GTO compliance — mandatory BO disclosure for all-cash purchases ≥$300K in covered markets
  • Title company AML program
  • Deed-of-trust / mortgage lender BSA compliance
  • Real estate attorney/agent SAR filing obligation (proposed FinCEN rule 2024)
  • BO matches or is associated with a designated individual
  • GTO threshold met with LLC purchaser and no BO disclosure (applies in FinCEN GTO-covered markets; see current GTO for applicable jurisdictions)
  • Linked to known narco or corruption proceeds investigation
  • SAR → FinCEN (covered institutions)
  • FinCEN GTO mandatory reporting
  • DOJ/FBI task force referral
  • IRS-CI referral for tax evasion nexus
 BSA / FinCEN GTO (31 USC §5326) · IEEPA / OFAC for sanctioned BO · 18 USC §1956 · Proposed FinCEN real estate AML rule (2024 ANPRM)
Luxury Goods / Art / Collectibles ML / Sanctions Evasion / CPF High-value art used as anonymous store of value; anonymous auction sales; shell company purchases of paintings, sculpture, watches, jewelry; DPRK art export proceeds; freeport storage (Geneva, Singapore, Luxembourg) as indefinite layering vehicle; antiquities trafficking for TF
  • Freeport-domiciled sale/purchase with no title chain — may present elevated opacity risk
  • Auction purchase by anonymous shell bidder
  • Rapid re-sale post-acquisition without market-based justification
  • Consignor linked to sanctioned jurisdiction
  • Artwork origin documentation absent or falsified
  • Art dealer/auction house AML program (AMLA 2020 coverage)
  • BO disclosure for purchases >$10K (pending FinCEN rulemaking)
  • Provenance verification requirement
  • Freeport operator due diligence protocols
  • Sanctioned consignor/buyer identified
  • CITES violation (antiquities)
  • Freeport-stored asset linked to active forfeiture investigation
  • SAR → FinCEN (post-AMLA 2020 coverage of art market)
  • OFAC voluntary self-disclosure for sanctions exposure
  • HSI referral for cultural property / antiquities trafficking
 AMLA 2020 (31 USC §5312 expanded) · IEEPA / OFAC SDN list · National Stolen Property Act 18 USC §2314 · UNESCO 1970 Convention / CPIA
V. Trade-Based Money Laundering (TBML) & Proliferation Finance
Node TypeThreat Vector(s)Abuse ModeRed Flags / IndicatorsControlsEscalation TriggersReporting PathwayAuthority Basis
Trade Finance / Letters of Credit TBML / CPF / Sanctions Evasion Over/under-invoicing to transfer value across borders; phantom shipments; multiple invoicing for single shipment; commodity diversion (oil, dual-use goods) to sanctioned end-user; Iran/Russia/DPRK procurement networks using front companies; AECA/ITAR violations via commodity smuggling
  • Material deviation from established commodity pricing benchmarks (cross-referenced against Panjiva, UN Comtrade)
  • Country of origin inconsistent with commodity type or routing
  • Consignee is known front company or newly established entity
  • Payment terms unusually favorable (advance payment to high-risk jurisdiction)
  • Dual-use goods with civil stated end-use but military-grade specifications
  • Trade-Based ML Risk Assessment per FATF Guidance
  • Dual invoice cross-check (shipping docs vs. L/C vs. buyer invoice)
  • Commodity pricing database verification
  • End-User Certificate (EUC) verification for dual-use exports
  • SWIFT MT700 screening for sanctioned entity / jurisdiction
  • Shipment routed through Iran/Russia/DPRK-nexus transshipment hub as identified through public reporting or screening indicators
  • Dual-use goods detected without export license
  • Invoice manipulation — material variance from pricing benchmark confirmed, consistent with potential diversion typology
  • SAR → FinCEN
  • BIS Office of Export Enforcement (OEE) referral
  • OFAC referral for sanctions nexus
  • DOJ NSD Counterproliferation Section referral
 AECA 22 USC §2778 · EAR 15 CFR §730-774 · IEEPA 50 USC §1705 · FATF TBML Guidance (2020) · UN SC Resolution 2094 (DPRK)
Free Trade Zones (FTZs) Sanctions Evasion / TBML / CPF Re-export and transshipment to sanctioned jurisdictions without origin disclosure; dual-use goods transit under civil commercial cover; commingling of sanctioned and licit cargo; document falsification; DPRK coal/oil transshipment typology; Russia technology procurement via UAE/Turkey FTZs post-2022
  • Consignee is FTZ-based trading company with no operational history
  • Final destination country not disclosed or differs from shipping manifest
  • Cargo type inconsistent with declared end-user industry
  • Rapid re-export within 48–72 hours of arrival in FTZ without commercial justification
  • FTZ operator AML/CFT controls (FATF Rec. 25 for FTZs)
  • Customs authority AIS/AES data analytics for transshipment anomalies
  • Financial institution refusal to finance FTZ-based transactions without full supply chain transparency
  • Coordinated Customs-FIU information sharing
  • Cargo destined for Russia/Iran/DPRK as identified through public reporting or screening indicators
  • Dual-use goods without export license transiting FTZ
  • FTZ operator linked to prior enforcement action
  • SAR → FinCEN for financial institutions financing the trade
  • BIS/OEE referral
  • CBP / HSI referral
  • FinCEN FTZ advisory engagement
 EAR 15 CFR Part 736 · IEEPA · 19 USC §1595a (CBP seizure authority) · FATF Guidance on FTZ risks

IX. Money Services Businesses & Remittance
