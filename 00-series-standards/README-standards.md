# WP-2026 Series Standards

**Center for Competitive Statecraft and Strategic Policy**
**Collin George · March 2026**

---

This folder contains the governing standards documents for the WP-2026 independent policy research series. All papers in the series are produced against these standards. New companion documents and revisions must conform to both documents before publication.

---

## Documents

### [WP-2026-StyleGuide-v3.pdf](WP-2026-StyleGuide-v3.pdf)

**WP-2026 Series Style and Verbiage Guide · Version 3.0**

The authoritative production and editorial standard for the series. Covers:

- **Institutional Voice Standard** — five governing principles: precision over elegance, economy over comprehensiveness, structure as epistemic weight carrier, agent specificity, and sourced quantitative claims
- **Analytic Tradecraft** — anchored to ODNI Analytic Standards and CIA structured analysis guidance: the three-part sentence structure (attribution → judgment → limits), dual probability/confidence axis, approved verb taxonomy, approved analytic phrases, weak-to-strong conversion table, and three-category prohibited language taxonomy (prosecutorial certainty / emotive inflation / advocacy language)
- **Document Architecture** — mandatory page structure, Key Judgments format, descriptive section header convention, preferred section labels for assessment papers
- **Visual Design System** — color palette, typography, box type conventions, left-border accent standard
- **Source Tier System** — Tier 1 through Tier 3 with usage rules and inline citation format
- **Series Dependency Graph** — complete upstream/downstream reference for all papers
- **Pre-Publication Revision Checklist** — 23-item checklist applied before any SSRN, journal, or think tank submission

Editable master: [WP-2026-StyleGuide-v3.docx](WP-2026-StyleGuide-v3.docx)

Supersedes: Style Guide v1.0 and v2.0 (both March 2026).

### [WP-2026-CI-Assessment-v2.pdf](WP-2026-CI-Assessment-v2.pdf)

**Counterintelligence Exposure Assessment: WP-2026 Independent Policy Research Series · Version 2.0**

Open-source counterintelligence exposure assessment produced for the author's academic risk management and professional transparency purposes. Contains no classified information. All analysis derived from public repository content and established open-source CI methodology.

**Purpose of publication:** The publication of a rigorous self-assessment of an open-source research series' exposure profile is an act of professional transparency demonstrating the operational security awareness the series is designed to embody. Adversary utility ratings are analytically inferred assessments of what a sophisticated foreign OSINT collection unit could derive from the public repository content — not confirmed adversary collection activity.

Covers:
- Series-level aggregation risk assessment (Key Judgments: 5 numbered judgments with probability and confidence labels)
- Paper-by-paper risk matrix (13 papers rated CRITICAL / HIGH / MODERATE)
- Adversary-specific utility analysis (PRC MSS, Russian SVR/FSB, Iranian MOIS)
- Analytic method exposure assessment
- Source triangulation risk assessment
- Intelligence gap disclosure assessment
- Consolidated risk matrix
- Recommendations for future series design

Key finding: The series is assessed as already collected by adversary OSINT programs. Adversary awareness of the analytical architecture is unlikely to defeat the enforcement mechanisms the series documents, because those mechanisms operate through structural legal authorities and institutional capacity — not through information asymmetry.

Editable master: [WP-2026-CI-Assessment-v2.docx](WP-2026-CI-Assessment-v2.docx)

Supersedes: CI Assessment v1.0 (March 2026).
