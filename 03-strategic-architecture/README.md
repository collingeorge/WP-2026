## Document Summaries

### CONTAIN-01 — Long-Term Sanctions Containment Doctrine
Rejects regime collapse and coercive breakthrough as realistic success criteria for competitive statecraft campaigns. Four-component framework: Integrated Systemic Attrition Doctrine (ISAD), Sustainable Pressure and Alternatives Denial (SPAD), Dollar Enforcement Effectiveness Index (DEEI) with automatic downshift protocol, and 90-day operational observability cycle. Reads upstream from PERSIST-01; reads into MAXPRESS-01.

### MAXPRESS-01 — Maximum Pressure Execution Architecture
Translates CONTAIN-01 / PERSIST-01 strategic architecture into practitioner-ready execution. Addresses five structural gaps from prior maximum pressure campaigns. Pre-staging rolling 90-day readiness packages, simultaneous BIS/OFAC action as default, Lloyd's/P&I Club maritime reliability degradation mechanism. Target-specific parameters for Iran/IRGC, Russia/shadow fleet, PRC networks, Cuba/GAESA, Venezuela. Reads into ESCALATE-01 (`04-applied-enforcement`).

### TARIFF-01 v9.5 — US Tariff Architecture Post-*Learning Resources v. Trump*
**Current version: v9.5 — supersedes all prior versions.**

Post-*Learning Resources, Inc. v. Trump* (SCOTUS, Feb. 20, 2026) tariff legal authority architecture. The ruling foreclosed IEEPA as tariff authority on statutory interpretation grounds; the major questions doctrine analysis is a plurality position (Roberts, Gorsuch, Barrett), not a holding of the Court. Operative authority set post-ruling: AD/CVD, Section 232, Section 301, Section 122.

Key Judgments include: post-SCOTUS tariff revenue bounded at ~$60–130 billion/year — a fraction of ~$970 billion FY2025 net interest, meaning tariffs cannot function as a primary debt-service instrument at any plausible post-ruling revenue level. The welfare-maximizing US tariff is approximately 9% incorporating the dollar's reserve currency role (Itskhoki-Mukhin 2025); effective post-SCOTUS rate is approximately 7–10.5%. A broad-based border-adjusted consumption tax would generate ~$230–350 billion annually with lower litigation and retaliation exposure.

Reads upstream from SCORES-01 (evidentiary foundation) and into FISCAL-01 (tariff revenue as input to consolidation architecture) and PERSIST-01 (IEEPA authority rail — *Learning Resources* constrains the IEEPA sanctions authority PERSIST-01 governs).

### BRI-01 v4.1 — Grand Strategy Capstone
Six Key Judgments assessing the conditional strategic opportunity opened by the March 2026 Hormuz crisis. Identifies a US-led coalition corridor architecture — Indian Ocean / Persian Gulf transit routes — capable of competing with BRI at contestable nodes and institutionalizing transit governance through a Hormuz-Indian Ocean Forum. Reads into EST-01.

### EST-01 — IC-Style Strategic Estimate
IC-style strategic estimate derived from BRI-01. Six Key Judgments with probability assessments. Five scenario-conditioned outcome pathways with probability bands. Three competing alternative hypotheses. Ten-indicator signpost table for monitoring. Structured for policy-level consumption.

### FISCAL-01 — US Fiscal Sustainability
**Baseline, Instruments, and Consolidation Architecture.**

Independent fiscal policy analysis based on CBO, JCT, SSA, Treasury, IMF, GAO, and published academic literature. Every estimate classified as ★ official score, ◆ modeled estimate, or ◇ illustrative planning range per the scoring taxonomy defined in SCORES-01.

**The baseline diagnosis:** CBO projects federal debt held by the public rising from approximately 100% of GDP today to 156% by 2055. Net interest consumed approximately $970 billion (18.5% of federal revenue) in FY2025 and is projected to exceed $1 trillion in FY2026. OASI and HI trust funds projected to deplete in 2033. The structural problem is that expenditure growth outpaces revenue growth while interest compounds on a larger base — this is a structural divergence, not a cyclical problem.

**The consolidation requirement:** Stabilizing debt-to-GDP requires fiscal adjustment of approximately 4.5–5.0% of GDP (~$1.4–1.5 trillion annually). Comparable in magnitude to the largest historical consolidation episodes. Subject to a 10–20% interaction-effects discount. No single instrument closes it — at least 3–4 structural instruments must be enacted simultaneously. Growth policy improves feasibility but does not substitute for fiscal adjustment.

**Instrument analysis:** The paper analyzes each major instrument — VAT/consumption tax, individual income tax reform, corporate tax, Social Security and Medicare reform, defense and discretionary caps, IRS enforcement funding, tariff revenue — with yield estimates, distributional incidence, political feasibility, and implementation constraints. The IRS enforcement section corrects a prior analytical error: the marginal return from IRA-funded enforcement is ~$18–20 billion/year average, not ~$56–85 billion/year (the higher figure conflated total existing IRS enforcement collections with marginal revenue from additional funding).

**Series connection:** The enforcement architecture this series describes presupposes US capacity to sustain multi-year campaigns. RESILIENCE-01 (`08-resilience-foundation`) addresses industrial capacity. FISCAL-01 addresses fiscal capacity. Together they form the prerequisite layer for the credibility of all enforcement-layer strategies.

Reads upstream from SCORES-01 (evidentiary foundation) and TARIFF-01 (tariff revenue capacity as input). Reads into ESCALATE-01 (`04-applied-enforcement`) — fiscal constraints on OFAC resourcing, DOJ forfeiture funding, and BIS enforcement appropriations are upstream of ESCALATE-01's institutional throughput constraint layer. Reads into RESILIENCE-01 (`08-resilience-foundation`) as companion prerequisite-layer document. Reads into PERSIST-01 (`02-core-frameworks`) — fiscal sustainability as a guardrail condition.

### STRAT-NSCC-01 — PRC Defense-Industrial Enforcement Calibration
**Exploitation of PRC Defense-Industrial Intelligence and Calibration of U.S. Enforcement Architecture.**

Event-driven operational strategy memorandum responding to the publicly reported FlamingChina breach of China's National Supercomputing Center (NSCC) in Tianjin (February–April 2026). Structured to align with ODNI analytic standards. Eight Key Judgments with confidence labeling and TIER source tags. Three Lines of Effort: Financial Intelligence Targeting (Treasury/FinCEN/OFAC), Export Control Recalibration (Commerce/BIS), and Diplomatic Multilateralization (State/ISN/T). Twelve recommended enforcement actions mapped to specific statutory authorities, with eleven executable under existing delegated authorities and one requiring presidential direction. Decision matrix distinguishes delegated, rulemaking, multilateral, and presidential-decision authority types across 90/180/360-day timelines.

Includes: synchronization framework (FinCEN→BIS→State feedback loops), risk register (APA exposure, market stability, coalition durability, adversary autarky acceleration, counterintelligence risk), and Source Reliability and Bibliography appendix (Annex C) transparently disclosing 21 unique sources across four tiers with explicit reliability ratings. Annex B provides illustrative force posture implications pending independent SME validation.

**Key authorities cited:** IEEPA, USA PATRIOT Act Section 311, EAR (15 CFR §764.3), E.O. 13959/14032, E.O. 14105, FAR 9.4, Wassenaar Arrangement.

Reads upstream from UNIFIED-01 (`02-core-frameworks`) for enforcement integration logic and CYBER-01 for cybersecurity context. Cross-references CTF-01, CPF-02, and ARCH-03 (`04-applied-enforcement`) enforcement authorities. CI exposure assessed in CI-ENFORCE-01 (`00-governance`) — Moderate rating.

## Series Position

Documents in this folder translate PERSIST-01's authority framework and SIEGE-01's mechanism into domain-specific strategic architectures. They sit between the core frameworks layer (`02-core-frameworks`) and the applied enforcement layer (`04-applied-enforcement`).

Reading order within this folder: CONTAIN-01 → MAXPRESS-01 → TARIFF-01 → FISCAL-01 → STRAT-NSCC-01 → BRI-01 → EST-01. FISCAL-01 should be read after TARIFF-01 (which provides tariff revenue capacity as an input) and before ESCALATE-01 in `04-applied-enforcement`. STRAT-NSCC-01 can be read independently but benefits from prior familiarity with UNIFIED-01 (`02-core-frameworks`) and CYBER-01.
