## CONTAIN-01 — Long-Term Sanctions Containment Doctrine

**Series position:** Containment architecture. Reads upstream from PERSIST-01. Reads downstream into MAXPRESS-01 and ESCALATE-01.

Rejects regime collapse and coercive breakthrough as realistic primary success criteria for sustained enforcement campaigns. Establishes a four-component framework:

- **Integrated Systemic Attrition Doctrine (ISAD):** Long-horizon cost imposition strategy targeting financial infrastructure capacity, not short-term behavioral change
- **Sustainable Pressure and Alternatives Denial (SPAD):** Architecture for maintaining enforcement pressure while denying adversary off-ramp pathways that would reconstitute capacity
- **Dollar Enforcement Effectiveness Index (DEEI):** Four-indicator composite index for assessing whether enforcement pressure remains within the operationally effective band; includes automatic downshift protocol when indicators fall below threshold
- **90-Day Operational Observability Cycle:** Rolling review cadence with mandatory falsifiable indicator assessment before each cycle extension

The DEEI automatic downshift protocol is one of the series' core contributions to enforcement discipline — it prevents the institutional tendency to continue enforcement campaigns after analytical support has degraded.

## MAXPRESS-01 — Maximum Pressure Execution Architecture (v4)

**Series position:** Execution layer. Translates CONTAIN-01 and PERSIST-01 into practitioner-ready enforcement architecture. Reads downstream into ESCALATE-01.

Addresses five structural gaps identified in prior maximum pressure campaigns:

1. Lack of pre-staged rolling 90-day readiness packages preventing rapid response
2. Sequential rather than simultaneous BIS/OFAC action, allowing substitution
3. Insufficient maritime reliability degradation through Lloyd's/P&I Club mechanisms
4. Absence of coalition fracture monitoring with automatic pause triggers
5. No compressed timeline mode for crisis-driven enforcement acceleration

Target-specific parameters developed for: Iran/IRGC, Russia/shadow fleet infrastructure, PRC-facilitated networks, Cuba/GAESA, Venezuela/PDVSA-adjacent networks.

## TARIFF-01 — Litigation-Resilient Tariff Enforcement Architecture (ANCHOR)

**Series position:** Tariff enforcement architecture. Reads upstream from PERSIST-01 authority rails. Independent from CONTAIN-01/MAXPRESS-01 — tariff enforcement as a distinct coercion instrument.

Seven authority rails with bright-line predicate definitions for each. Key contributions:

- **Explicit exclusion list:** Trade deficits and foreign exchange movements are insufficient predicates for IEEPA tariff action under the major questions doctrine framework established in *West Virginia v. EPA* — this exclusion is stated as a hard limit, not a caution
- **Five-indicator economic shock guardrail** with mandatory pause threshold
- **Appendix C:** 70/30/24-month strategic dependency threshold analysis — the three-horizon framework for identifying when tariff pressure reaches the supply chain reconstitution window

The ANCHOR designation reflects the paper's function as the fixed legal and evidentiary foundation for tariff-based coercion within the PERSIST architecture.

## BRI-01 — The Corridor Wager: Grand Strategy Capstone (v4.1)

**Series position:** Grand strategy layer. Produced in the context of the March 2026 Hormuz crisis. Reads upstream from PERSIST-01 and CONTAIN-01. Reads downstream into EST-01.

**Central argument:** The March 2026 Hormuz crisis opened a conditional strategic opportunity window — a US-led coalition corridor architecture is capable of competing with BRI at contestable nodes if the window is acted upon before IRGC leverage is reconstituted and before Chinese alternative financing fills the infrastructure gap.

Six Key Judgments with dual-axis probability and confidence labels:

1. The corridor architecture is technically feasible within a defined coalition set
2. The Hormuz-Indian Ocean Forum is the institutional vehicle that converts bilateral agreements into a durable governance structure
3. BRI contestability is node-specific, not system-wide — the analysis identifies which nodes are genuinely contestable
4. The window is time-bounded by IRGC reconstitution timeline and Chinese financing acceleration
5. Coalition sequencing matters — wrong sequencing order forecloses nodes that correct sequencing keeps open
6. The strategic cost of inaction exceeds the strategic cost of commitment

## EST-01 — IC-Style Strategic Estimate

**Series position:** Strategic estimate derivative of BRI-01. Written in IC analytic register.

Applies the IC-style estimate format to the BRI-01 strategic assessment:
- Six Key Judgments with probability assessments stated in ODNI confidence lexicon
- Five scenario-conditioned outcome pathways with probability bands
- Three competing alternative hypotheses with explicit evidence thresholds for revision
- Ten-indicator signpost table for monitoring which scenario pathway is materializing

EST-01 demonstrates the analytical mobility of the BRI-01 framework — the same strategic assessment produces both a policy paper (BRI-01) and an IC-format estimate (EST-01) without contradiction, reflecting the series' compliance with ODNI analytic standards throughout.

## MAXPRESS-IMMIG-01 — Maximum Pressure Immigration Architecture (v6)

**Series position:** Applied maximum pressure architecture for immigration-linked transnational criminal network enforcement.

Applies the MAXPRESS-01 architecture and PERSIST-01 authority rails to immigration enforcement contexts — specifically targeting the financial and operational infrastructure of transnational criminal organizations that exploit immigration pathways. Addresses IEEPA authority, Treasury/DHS coordination architecture, and the enforcement instruments available at the intersection of immigration law and sanctions authority.
