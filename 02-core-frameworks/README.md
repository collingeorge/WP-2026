# 02-core-frameworks — Core Analytical Frameworks

This folder contains the mechanism layer of the WP-2026 series — the documents that define how enforcement works, where it fails, how adversaries respond, and what the system looks like against the hardest real-world case. These seven documents function as an integrated stack. SIEGE-01 is the axis; every other document in this folder is either a prerequisite (PERSIST-01), a companion (UNIFIED-01, EVASION-01, COUNTERINTEL-01), or a validation (CI-PLATFORM-01).

Read PERSIST-01 before SIEGE-01. Read EVASION-01 and COUNTERINTEL-01 after SIEGE-01 and before CI-PLATFORM-01.

## PERSIST-01 — Competitive Statecraft Architecture

**Series position:** Authority layer. All enforcement papers import authority analysis from PERSIST-01 by cross-reference. No series paper independently analyzes statutory authority.

**Exclusive ownership domains:**
- Six authority rails: Export Controls, Sanctions (IEEPA/OFAC), CFIUS, Defense Cooperation, Speech/Information, and International Law/Alliance — including hard limits, drafting rules, and FDPR extraterritoriality boundaries
- Tiered escalation ladder (Tier 0–3) with Coalition-Risk Review requirements at Tier 2+
- Guardrail Index with four systemic stability indicators and Override Memorandum procedure
- Four-tier standards of proof: Credible Signal, Confirmed Attribution, Material Enabling Behavior, Systemic-Risk Threshold
- Neutral designation-selection rule: objective triggers, prohibited factor list, and designation-selection log as the primary defense exhibit against selective-enforcement challenge
- Coalition governance doctrine: coalition-first sequencing as a durability rule; allied concurrence standards
- Model record artifacts: Designation Memorandum, Coalition-Risk Review Memorandum, Delisting/Off-Ramp Memorandum, Guardrail Override Memorandum

## SIEGE-01 — Multi-Channel Financial Denial Framework (Final)

**Series position:** Core mechanism paper. Owns the analytical claims that all other enforcement papers import. SAM-01 designates SIEGE-01 as the series governing framework on mechanism theory.

**Exclusive ownership domains:**
- The seven-channel financial access disruption framework and the rationale for why sequential designation fails
- Why simultaneous multi-channel pressure is analytically different from sequential enforcement
- Network tier classification system: Tier A (institutionalized, state-adjacent), Tier B (semi-institutionalized), Tier C (opportunistic/adaptive)
- Cumulative cost-escalation logic — the mechanism by which simultaneous channel pressure produces compounding disruption
- The fragmentation governing finding: fragmentation without parallel organizational disruption is a probable adverse outcome — series-wide binding, all papers must adopt this assessment
- Failure mode analysis for each of the seven channels
- Falsifiable observable indicators and mandatory analytical response protocol
- Non-transferability rule: this framework is not applicable across targets without independent gate assessment of all five pre-deployment conditions

**SAM-01 compliance note:** SIEGE-01 Final resolves two active contradictions identified in SAM-01 Rev 2.0 — the Category 3 fragmentation contradiction with the prior UNIFIED-01, and the Category 2 authority mechanics redundancy. Both are resolved in this version.

## UNIFIED-01 — Cross-Domain Financial Enforcement Synthesis (v3)

**Series position:** Cross-domain application memo. Reads with SIEGE-01, PERSIST-01, and SAM-01. Owns exclusively the cross-domain synthesis layer.

**Exclusive ownership domains:**
- The cross-domain shared infrastructure doctrine: cartels, proliferation networks, and terrorist financing facilitators use the same shadow financial nodes, and single designation actions produce cascading enforcement effects across multiple threat domains simultaneously
- The mapping of which statutory tools produce cross-domain cascading results and in what combination
- The threat-based decision framework: which primary and supporting frameworks apply to each threat indicator type
- Cross-domain interagency coordination structure at bureau level across narcotics, terrorist financing, and proliferation finance simultaneously

**Prohibited content (per SAM-01 Part V):** UNIFIED-01 may not describe how the seven channels operate mechanically, restate the Tier A/B/C classification logic, provide quantitative outcome projections, or develop implementation sequencing. All such content belongs to SIEGE-01 or PERSIST-01 and must be imported by cross-reference.

**SAM-01 compliance note:** v3 resolves the fragmentation contradiction (adopts SIEGE-01's governing finding) and the authority mechanics redundancy (defers all operative mechanics to SIEGE-01 Part V).

## EVASION-01 — Adversary Adaptation Architecture (Final)

**Series position:** Active adversary model. Companion to COUNTERINTEL-01. Cross-referenced by SIEGE-01 Parts VII and V.

**Analytical function:** Maps how adaptive illicit finance networks actively exploit enforcement framework boundaries — by domain, condition, and time horizon. Organized to give enforcement framework designers a systematic map of adversary behavior so that architecture is designed against realistic adversarial responses rather than ideal conditions.

**Analytical distinction from COUNTERINTEL-01:** EVASION-01 answers how adversaries actively exploit degradation conditions. COUNTERINTEL-01 answers where and when the framework degrades passively. The two documents are complementary, not redundant — active exploitation versus passive constraint.

**Scope:** Descriptive, not prescriptive. Does not provide a guide to evading enforcement actions. Purpose is adversarial completeness in enforcement framework design and prevention of false-positive effectiveness assessments.

## COUNTERINTEL-01 — Framework Degradation Conditions (Final)

**Series position:** Passive degradation map. Constraint layer for SIEGE-01. Cross-referenced by SIEGE-01 Parts III, V, VI, X, XI.4 — the most structurally integrated document in the folder after SIEGE-01 itself.

**Analytical function:** Defines the conditions under which the multi-channel enforcement framework loses analytical support — where the cumulative logic SIEGE-01 requires is not analytically operative under current conditions. Distinguishes between "framework succeeds" and "framework's analytical support degrades" — a critical distinction because a degraded deployment may still produce some enforcement effect while no longer meeting the cumulative logic threshold.

**Original contribution:** Treating degraded deployment as producing the full framework effect is an assessment error that leads to false conclusions about network capacity, misallocation of enforcement resources, and incorrect predictions about adversary behavior. COUNTERINTEL-01 provides the detection architecture for identifying when that error is being made.

## CI-PLATFORM-01 — Lazarus Group Gate Assessment and Empirical Stress Test

**Series position:** Case application — first empirical stress test of the series. Applies SIEGE-01 gate framework and EVASION-01 adversary model to a real-world target profile.

**Why Lazarus Group:** Lazarus Group constitutes the analytically hardest boundary condition for the SIEGE-01 framework. It is a state-adjacent network that has completed Phase 3 institutionalization of financial infrastructure outside the USD-correspondent perimeter, operates through channels SIEGE-01 identifies as structurally uncontrollable, and has demonstrated adversary adaptation rates that compress the simultaneity window below the preparation timeline the framework requires.

**Analytical purpose:** This is not a targeting recommendation or enforcement proposal. Its purpose is to assess whether SIEGE-01's analytical architecture — specifically its Tier classification system, gate conditions, failure mode logic, and stated non-capabilities — accurately characterizes the enforcement environment the published record describes. It does.

**Finding:** The Lazarus Group financial architecture meets the Tier C classification criteria. The gate assessment confirms that the framework's non-capability claims are accurate for this target profile, and that CI-PLATFORM-01 defines the outer boundary of what the series can analytically support for state-adjacent networks.

## Feedback Loop — SIEGE-01 / EVASION-01 / COUNTERINTEL-01 / CI-PLATFORM-01

These four documents have an explicit analytical feedback relationship not fully captured by the linear dependency graph:

1. **COUNTERINTEL-01** defines degradation conditions — where the framework loses analytical support
2. **EVASION-01** maps how adversaries actively exploit those degradation conditions
3. **CI-PLATFORM-01** validates both against a real target — confirming which degradation conditions and exploitation patterns are empirically observable in the Lazarus Group case
4. The findings from CI-PLATFORM-01 inform the boundary conditions stated in SIEGE-01's gate framework

This loop is the empirical validation architecture of the series. It distinguishes WP-2026 from frameworks that define mechanism without testing limits.
