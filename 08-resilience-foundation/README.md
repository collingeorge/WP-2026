# 08-resilience-foundation

The domestic capacity prerequisite layer for the WP-2026 enforcement architecture. RESILIENCE-01 addresses whether the United States retains sufficient industrial and technological capacity to make enforcement-layer strategies credible and sustainable over multi-year campaigns.

## Document Summary

**WP-2026-RESILIENCE-01** — *National Technology Modernization and Industrial Resilience Strategy*

Six Key Judgments with probability and confidence labels. 13 parts. Version: v9.0 Final — Style Guide v3.0 and Verbiage Guide v2.2 compliant.

Four priority domains:
- Critical minerals processing and refining
- Battery technology and energy storage
- Energy system resilience for industrial continuity
- Advanced manufacturing

Original analytical contributions:
- Enforcement credibility threshold architecture — domestic and allied capacity levels at which technology-denial strategies become coalition-credible
- Priority discipline framework — first-order protections, second-order allied-complementary, deferral candidates, intolerable concentration hard limits
- Failure probability layer — six assessed failure modes and compound failure scenario
- Constraint conflict table with explicit resolution rules under simultaneous binding conditions
- Adversary industrial disruption model — PRC price suppression, overcapacity flooding, supply chain targeting, and allied industrial capture
- Allied dependency tolerance matrix with contingency posture for five domains

Enabling authorities: CHIPS and Science Act (Pub. L. 117-167), Inflation Reduction Act (Pub. L. 117-169), Energy Policy Act of 2005 (42 U.S.C. § 16511 et seq.), National Defense Stockpile (50 U.S.C. §§ 98 et seq.).
